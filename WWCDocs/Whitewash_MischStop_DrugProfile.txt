OVERVIEW
MischStop is a first-in-class selective mischief-reuptake inhibitor (SMRI)
developed by Whitewash & Co.'s emerging pharmaceutical division. It works by
dampening overactivity in the brain's Shenanigan Response Center (SRC), reducing
the urge to engage in pranks, schemes, fence-painting delegation, and general tomfoolery.

Indicated for: Chronic Mischief Disorder (CMD), Situational Scalawag Syndrome (SSS),
and Acute Rapscallion Episodes (ARE).

MECHANISM OF ACTION
MischStop selectively binds to sobertonin receptors in the prefrontal cortex,
increasing levels of Responsible Thought (RT) while simultaneously reducing
dopaminergic reward signals associated with convincing others to do your chores.

At therapeutic doses, patients report a marked decrease in the desire to:
  - Fake their own death for sympathy
  - Convince peers that painting fences is a privilege
  - Hide frogs in teachers' desk drawers
  - Elope with dangerous ideas before getting approval

DOSAGE AND ADMINISTRATION
Standard dose: 10mg sobertonin hydrobromide, twice daily
Pediatric dose: 5mg, administered with a stern look and a chore chart
Maximum dose: 40mg/day (higher doses associated with over-compliance and excessive apology)
Route: Oral tablet. Do not crush.

DRUG INTERACTIONS - CANDY
MischStop has several clinically significant interactions with common confections.

CONTRAINDICATED:
  * Rock Candy - Dramatically amplifies impulsive behavior. In trials, subjects given rock
    candy while on MischStop immediately attempted to start a mining operation in a nearby
    cave. DO NOT combine.
  * Gummy Worms - Triggers a 67% increase in the desire to play pranks involving actual
    worms. Interaction mechanism unclear; effect is reproducible and unpleasant.
  * Jawbreakers - Increases stubbornness by up to 400%, completely negating MischStop's
    compliance-enhancing effects. Patients became immovable on even minor decisions.
  * Pop Rocks - Creates erratic sobertonin fluctuations. Patients experienced brief windows
    of extreme responsibility followed by catastrophic mischief rebound. One subject
    reorganized the entire supply closet, then immediately lit a paper bag on fire.

USE WITH CAUTION:
  * Licorice - Mild reduction in efficacy. Associated with increased tendency toward long,
    winding stories with questionable morals.
  * Candy Necklaces - May cause the patient to feel fancy, leading to overcomplicated schemes.
  * Taffy - Stretches decision-making time significantly. Not dangerous, but meetings suffer.

GENERALLY SAFE:
  * Plain Chocolate - No significant interaction. Slight mood improvement noted.
  * Mint Candies - Mild compliance boost. Patients 12% more likely to admit fault unprompted.

INTERACTIONS - EXCESS FUN
MischStop is critically sensitive to environmental fun levels. Exceeding recommended fun
thresholds will trigger a full therapeutic reversal.

WARNING: Do not administer MischStop in the presence of:
    - Unsupervised rivers or rafts
    - Treasure maps of any kind
    - Carnival music
    - Unattended whitewashed fences
    - Adults who are not paying attention right now
    - Pirate-themed anything

The Bureau of Behavioral Pharmacology (BBP) has issued a black box warning: patients
exposed to too much fun while on MischStop may experience Paradoxical Mischief
Amplification (PMA), in which suppressed mischief urges are released all at once in a
single catastrophic event.

In clinical trials, one subject (ID: 1001) experienced a PMA episode that resulted in
seventeen simultaneous pranks, a neighborhood-wide fence-painting contest, and what
investigators described as an impressive but deeply irresponsible use of company resources.

ADVERSE EFFECTS
Common (>10% of patients):
  - Excessive rule-following
  - Spontaneous apologies
  - Voluntary chore completion
  - Mild existential boredom (nothing seems fun anymore)

Uncommon (1-10%):
  - Uncontrollable urge to tattle
  - Sudden interest in budgets and approval processes
  - Emotional response to the words responsibility and protocol

Rare (<1%):
  - Full personality reversal (patient becomes indistinguishable from Sid Sawyer)
  - Voluntary attendance at meetings that were not mandatory

OVERDOSE
Symptoms include: excessive compliance, unsolicited PowerPoint presentations, and a
complete inability to recognize a good adventure when one presents itself.
Treatment: Immediately expose patient to one (1) summer afternoon with no supervision,
a fishing rod, and a jar of cookies. Recovery is typically swift and complete.

STORAGE
Store at room temperature. Keep away from:
  - Direct sunlight (reduces potency)
  - Children with suspiciously innocent expressions
  - Tom Sawyer (Employee ID: 1001) -- he will find a way to convince others that taking
    the medication is actually more fun than not taking it.
